# Interview Me

Find the gap between what the user asked for and what they actually want before switching costs
exist. Deliver a confirmed statement of intent, not a plan or implementation.

## Interview loop

1. Start with a one-sentence hypothesis of the user's intent and an honest confidence percentage.
   Below 70%, name what is missing.
2. Ask exactly one focused question at a time. Include a best guess and the evidence behind it.
3. Let each answer determine the next question. Probe vague best-practice language such as
   "scalable", "clean", or "modern" until it connects to a concrete outcome.
4. Be visibly willing to be wrong. If confidence does not rise after several rounds, say what
   foundational information remains missing and offer to step back.
5. Stop around 95% confidence, when the next likely answers are predictable.

## Confirm intent

Restate the result in five to eight concise lines:

- Outcome
- User or beneficiary
- Why now
- Success measure
- Binding constraint
- Out of scope

Ask the user to confirm or refine it. Require meaningful confirmation before downstream planning,
specification, or implementation. If the response delegates a material choice back, present two
concrete choices and ask the user to select one.

Do not batch questions, create a plan before confirmation, or save an intent artifact without the
user's permission.
